# LDOA Operational Language Concepts v1.0
## Beings, Promises, Authority, and Speech Acts

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the conceptual distinctions that support LDOA example flows.

It is not tied to the potato sale example.

It preserves the general rules behind the flow:

```text
Beings attend Situations.
Domains do not attend Situations.
Promises determine what Beings may attend.
Authority determines the force of what Beings may produce.
Speech Acts are the operational language produced by Beings.
Declarations transform operational reality when authority exists.
Affirmations coordinate, confirm, report, or leave trace.
Breakdowns become new Situations.
Operational continuity is maintained through attended Situations and produced Speech Acts.
```

---

## 2. Beings Attend, Not Domains

In LDOA, domains do not attend Situations.

Beings attend Situations.

A Being attends a Situation when that Situation falls within its promise.

The domain provides context, boundaries, responsibility, and authority conditions, but the domain itself is not the listener.

A Being may live in one domain and operate over Situations that appear in another domain, as long as attending those Situations falls within its promise.

However, producing a Declaration with transformative force in a domain still requires authority for that domain.

The distinction is therefore:

```text
Promise determines what a Being may attend.

Authority determines the operational force of what a Being may produce.

Domain provides the context and boundary where that authority has meaning.
```

The movement is:

```text
Situation becomes available
→ a Being attends it if it falls within its promise
→ the Being contextualizes it according to its promise, operation, and relevant domain context
→ the Being produces a Speech Act
→ another Being may attend that Speech Act if it falls within its promise
```

A domain does not listen.

A Being listens, attends, judges, and produces Speech Acts.

---

## 3. Promise and Attention

A promise determines what a Being may attend.

A Being does not attend everything.

A Being attends a Situation or Speech Act only when it falls within its promise.

A Being may be designed by the business to attend Situations across different domains, if those Situations fall within its promise.

This means that attention is not limited by domain ownership alone.

The domain provides context and authority boundaries.

The promise determines the Being’s attention boundary.

---

## 4. Authority and Operational Force

Authority determines the operational force of what a Being may produce.

This is especially important for Declarations.

A Being may attend Situations across different domains when its promise allows it.

However, a Being cannot declare with transformative force in any domain unless it has authority in that domain.

A Declaration transforms operational reality only when produced with the required authority.

Without authority, a produced act may still affirm, report, coordinate, or expose a Breakdown, but it does not transform that domain’s operational reality as a Declaration.

---

## 5. Speech Acts as Operational Language

In LDOA, Beings do not communicate directly with other Beings.

A Being attends a Situation when that Situation falls within its promise.

After attending it, the Being produces a Speech Act.

That Speech Act may be:

```text
Affirmation
Declaration
Breakdown
```

Other Beings do not listen to the Being itself.

They attend the Speech Act produced by that Being only if that Speech Act falls within their own promise.

Speech Acts are the operational language produced by Beings after attending Situations.

They are not direct communication between Beings.

They are the produced acts that become available for further attention.

The movement is therefore:

```text
Situation falls within a Being’s promise
→ Being attends the Situation
→ Being produces a Speech Act
→ Speech Act becomes available
→ another Being attends it if it falls within its promise
→ operational continuity continues
```

---

## 6. Declaration

A **Declaration** transforms operational reality when the Being has authority within the relevant domain or area.

A Declaration can open, close, transform, authorize, or make available a new operational condition.

For example:

```text
Sales Declaration:
The sale was registered / accepted in the Sales domain.
```

If produced by a Sales Being with authority, this Declaration changes the world of the Sales domain.

The sale now exists as registered or accepted in that domain.

A Being may not declare with transformative force in a domain where it lacks authority.

---

## 7. Affirmation

An **Affirmation** confirms, reports, coordinates, or leaves trace of something that was attended.

It does not transform operational reality by itself.

An Affirmation can still provide operational continuity.

Its continuity is informational, coordinative, or confirmatory.

For example:

```text
Affirmation:
The sale registration was completed.
```

This may allow another Being to inform a human operator:

```text
Your sale was registered.
```

The response does not register the sale again.

It confirms or reports that the sale was registered.

---

## 8. Breakdown

A **Breakdown** exposes that a Being could not continue or fulfill its promise.

A Breakdown does not deny the original situation.

It creates a new Situation to be attended.

For example:

```text
Breakdown:
The sale could not be registered because the product was not recognized.
```

That Breakdown becomes a new Situation.

Another Being may attend it if it falls within its promise.

Continuity does not mean that everything succeeds.

Continuity means that what happens remains attendable.

---

## 9. Declaration, Affirmation, and Breakdown Compared

The difference between Declaration, Affirmation, and Breakdown is not only in the text of the act.

The difference depends on:

```text
who produces the act
what promise the Being is fulfilling
what authority the Being has
in which area or domain the act is produced
whether the act transforms operational reality
whether the act confirms, reports, coordinates, or leaves trace
what continuity the act enables
```

The same sentence may have different force depending on who produces it, under what promise, and with what authority.

For example:

```text
“The sale was registered.”
```

Inside the Sales domain, produced by a Sales Being with authority, this may be a Declaration.

When communicated back to the human operator, the same content may be an Affirmation.

It does not register the sale again.

It confirms that the sale was registered.

---

## 10. Downstream Beings

Other Beings do not need to attend the original human expression or transport message.

They attend the Speech Acts produced by previous Beings if those Speech Acts fall within their promise.

Those Beings may live or operate in other domains.

For example, after a Sales Declaration:

```text
An Inventory Being may attend stock impact.
An Accounting Being may attend accounting impact.
A Registration Being may attend traceability.
A Reporting Being may attend reporting impact.
```

Each Being attends according to its own promise.

Each Being may affirm, declare, or break down.

No Being should declare outside its authority.

The chain of continuity is produced through Speech Acts, not through hidden mutation.

---

## 11. Annulment, Replacement, and Reframing

LDOA does not correct reality by silently mutating the past.

When an operational correction is needed, a new Situation is produced.

That new Situation may declare that a previous Situation is annulled, replaced, superseded, or reframed.

For example:

```text
Original Situation:
A sale was declared and registered.

New Situation:
The previous Sales Declaration must be corrected.

Correction Declaration:
The previous Sales Declaration was annulled.
A corrected Sales Declaration was produced.
```

The original Situation remains part of the operational trace.

The corrected Situation becomes the new active operational path.

This preserves both:

```text
historical traceability
operational continuity
```

Nothing is silently updated.  
Nothing is overwritten.  
Nothing disappears.

A previous Situation may be annulled only through a new Situation.

---

## 12. Central Principle

Operational reality occurs independently of what the system records, validates, or returns.

If something occurred in reality, that operational reality must not disappear because the system failed to transcribe, interpret, classify, persist, validate, or complete a promise.

At the same time, downstream operational continuity depends on Speech Acts produced by Beings.

LDOA distinguishes:

```text
A situation occurred.
A human or Being expressed it.
The situation became treatable.
A Being attended it because it fell within its promise.
The Being contextualized it using relevant operational and domain context.
The Being fulfilled or attempted to fulfill its promise.
The Being produced a Declaration, Affirmation, or Breakdown.
Other Beings attended the produced Speech Act only if it fell within their promise.
Operational continuity was maintained.
```

A failure does not erase the operational act.

It creates a new Situation to be attended.

If a correction is required, the correction also enters the system as a new Situation.

---

## 13. Continuity, Not Perfection

LDOA does not assume that operational reality can be perfectly modeled.

It assumes that reality will exceed the model.

The purpose is not to prevent every failure.

The purpose is to make failures attendable without collapsing the operation.

A Being may fail to fulfill its promise.

A Being may lack authority to declare in a domain.

A message may be ambiguous.

A business condition may be incomplete.

None of those conditions should erase the operational situation.

They should become addressable.

Operational continuity is preserved when the system can continue attending situations, even when a specific path fails, breaks down, is delegated, is annulled, is affirmed, is declared, or must be reframed.

---

## 14. Transport Independence

Transport is not essential to the pattern.

The same situation could start from any transport mechanism capable of carrying an expression of operational reality.

LDOA does not prescribe the transport mechanism.

What matters is that the situation can be expressed, attended, made treatable, contextualized, declared, affirmed, broken down, delegated, and continued within the ontological domain of language.

A concrete implementation may use any suitable transport mechanism.

The ontology remains the same.

---

## 15. Implementation Note

BODY may implement this pattern using internal Beings, domains, layers, channels, and technical capabilities.

Those internal design decisions are outside the scope of the public example flow.

The reader should focus on the architectural behavior:

```text
situations are attended when they fall within a Being’s promise
domains do not attend situations; Beings do
Beings produce Speech Acts after attending situations
Declarations transform operational reality when authority exists
Affirmations coordinate, confirm, report, or leave trace
Breakdowns become new Situations
other Beings attend produced Speech Acts when they fall within their promise
continuity is maintained
```
